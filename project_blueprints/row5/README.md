# Defense Doctrine — defensive retrieval governance laboratory (claim-limit discipline)

## Public Pitch

**What it is.** A defensive-RAG governance doctrine whose first job is to stop immature security claims from inflating themselves.

**Distinctive mechanism.** Every claim must carry mission profile, attacker tier, objective class, evidence packet, current boundary, residual risk, and downgrade triggers before it can mature through C1 Designed, C2 Implemented, C3 Validated, C4 Adaptively Stress-Tested, C5 Operationally Sustainable, and C6 Externally Defensible stages, with NIST AI RMF, NIST SP, and Google SAIF-style controls as reference discipline.

**Why it matters.** Its value is the language firewall: it makes words like secure, robust, solved, government-grade, and production-ready unavailable until evidence earns them.

### Built components

Every control in the lab currently sits at the first lifecycle gate — Designed. Nothing has been promoted to Implemented, because nothing is yet implemented; the lab has chosen to wait rather than promote on documentation alone. This is the discipline working on its own author, not a confession of weakness. The five live claims describe what the lab is right now: a first-draft governance pack, a limited model of the control plane, a model of provenance and transformation and chunk lineage, a limited set of runtime-governance rules, and a program-level register with ownership and evaluation doctrine. Each is documentary in nature and is held at the documentary lifecycle level. None of them yet says the lab does anything operational. The first experiment the lab has scheduled, EXP-001, points at the lab itself — attacker tier T0 (the lab's own personnel), objective O6 (claim inflation / governance bypass). Before defending anything outside, the lab defends against the most likely failure mode of any methodology paper, which is its own author overstating it. And the lab has formally pre-published what it is not: no code yet exists; no real adversary emulation has been run; no incident has been recorded against the I1-I8 taxonomy; only the lowest attacker tier is exercised; only one of the six adversary objectives is exercised; no comparator run, no adaptive evaluation, and no assurance case beyond the template have been produced. These are open questions, and writing them down is itself one of the lab's controls.

### Source use

Source use is organized by role: method authorities, domain evidence, technical background, implementation evidence, and claim limits. The page should show why each source matters; direct quotations and stronger factual claims still require exact passage anchors before publication.

### Plan

The lab is not waiting to write more doctrine. It is waiting to acquire a real defensive operation to govern, and then to graduate the controls one at a time by producing the kind of evidence each gate demands. The order is fixed by what the lab is structurally waiting for. Step 01 Anti-Distillation online Row 03 prototype has to come online first — until it exists, the lab has nothing real to govern, and any promotion above C1 would be the lab inflating its own claims. Step 02 Designed — Implemented Each control promoted by being tied to a Primary Work Product drawn from a real operation, not by restatement. Step 03 Eight-field evidence packet Every promotion accompanied by all eight mandatory fields, or the lab's own register rejects the move. Step 04 Implemented — Validated Limited evidence that the control behaves as specified under stated conditions. Step 05 Adaptively stress-tested Evidence under conditions the lab did not pre-publish. Step 06 Quarterly review cadence Activated when there is real operational behavior to review; until then unscheduled, and that fact lives in the register.
