# Regional Studies — eight place-based analytical pages (Africa, Berlin, East Asia, London, Mideast, Mongol, Russia, Western)

## Public Pitch

**What it is.** A regional-studies index that organizes place-based pages as analytical workbenches rather than travelogue or topic summaries.

**Distinctive mechanism.** It separates each regional page by case scope, source spine, historical inheritance, security relevance, and remaining verification work, with Russia Desk treated as the deepest current model.

**Why it matters.** It helps a reader see which regional pages are mature analytical rooms and which remain thinner case surfaces needing bibliography and source-lineage work.

## Blueprint

### Mechanism

### Built components

The eight regional study pages are represented here as a native index. Russia Desk is the deepest with the two-page split (Security Desk + History Page) and the twenty-five-seat governing faculty. Berlin, London, Mideast, and Western carry substantial scholarly content; Africa, East Asia, and Mongol carry structural skeletons that continue to grow.

### Source use

Source use is organized by role: method authorities, domain evidence, technical background, implementation evidence, and claim limits. The page should show why each source matters; direct quotations and stronger factual claims still require exact passage anchors before publication.

### Plan

Continue building case-study and bibliographic depth across the pages still at skeleton level (Africa, East Asia, Mongol). Bind every regional claim to its source register through the same source-register pipeline the rest of the portfolio is moving toward. Once the binding lands across all eight surfaces, the regional pillar becomes a uniformly source-bound analytical layer rather than a varied-density set of essays.
