# Analyst Workstation — eight-page interactive AI-augmented intelligence-analysis handbook

## Public Pitch

**What it is.** An analyst workstation for AI-augmented intelligence analysis.

**Distinctive mechanism.** It converts AI support into inspection surfaces: knowledge production, prompt engineering discipline, cognitive-bias safeguards, Key Assumptions Check, live SAT visualization instruments, source review, ICD/NIST-bound assurance language, analyst-model disagreement, and human acceptance or correction before conclusions move forward. The cross-page workstation can connect AI Governance Framework Navigator, EU AI Act considerations, AI System Assessment, AI Failure Mode Taxonomy, OSINT Narrative Mapping, OSIRIS LLM, and per-stage AI/HITL/ICD/NIST checks.

**Why it matters.** It matters because human-in-the-loop review only has value when the human has a concrete workstation for inspecting sources, uncertainty, assumptions, structured analytic technique outputs, and reasoning paths rather than a generic prompt window.

### Built components

All nine pages render at their intended fidelity. Each instrument page declares its expert roster and its citation count, and in aggregate the eight instrument pages reference more than five hundred citations across the major intelligence-studies and AI-governance authorities — Heuer, George, Lowenthal, Kent, Fingar, Bullock, Pashentsev, Beebe and Pherson, alongside the ODNI, NIST, EU AI Act, and Stanford HAI corpora. The citation infrastructure is in place; what is not yet built is the source-register chain that would bind each declared citation to an admitted snippet from the underlying corpus. The citations sit at an early stage where the citation infrastructure is in place but not yet bound to admitted source snippets, and the portfolio's governance overlay refuses stronger language than "declared" until that binding is built. The parent Analyst Assurance Desk renders the suite's coherent shell, but the cross-page evidence aggregator that would let an analyst see a SAT instrument in Page 7 trace back to its supporting Heuer passage in Page 1 is the next major build — it is the move that converts the suite from eight related instruments into a connected reasoning surface.

### Source use

Source use is organized by role: method authorities, domain evidence, technical background, implementation evidence, and claim limits. The page should show why each source matters; direct quotations and stronger factual claims still require exact passage anchors before publication.

### Plan

Graduate the suite from declared citations to provable citations — wire the source-register chain that the pages already announce as missing. The order is dictated by leverage: the densest pages get the binding work first, because that is where the discipline is most visible to a reader. Step 01 High-density pages first AI-Augmented Intelligence Cycle Synthesis and Knowledge Production — the densest pages — lifted first, where the source-register pilot has the most leverage. Step 02 Cross-page evidence aggregator Built so an analyst can follow a claim across pages — a SAT instrument in one page traces back to the Heuer passage that grounds it in another. Step 03 Six-page propagation Source-register binding propagated to the remaining six instrument pages so the suite becomes uniformly fully citation-bound. Step 04 Citation-roster verification Heuer / George / Lowenthal / Kent / Fingar / Bullock / Pashentsev / Beebe-Pherson / ODNI / NIST / EU AI Act / Stanford HAI all verified against the admitted-snippet store; declared roster becomes provable roster.
