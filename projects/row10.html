What this does

An analyst workstation for AI-augmented intelligence analysis.

Record note: project purpose

How it works

It converts AI support into inspection surfaces: knowledge production, prompt engineering discipline, cognitive-bias safeguards, Key Assumptions Check, live SAT visualization instruments, source review, ICD/NIST-bound assurance language, analyst-model disagreement, and human acceptance or correction before conclusions move forward.

Record note: working mechanism

The cross-page workstation can connect AI method discipline Framework Navigator, EU AI Act considerations, AI System Assessment, AI Failure Mode Taxonomy, OSINT Narrative Mapping, OSIRIS LLM, and per-stage AI/HITL/ICD/NIST checks.

Record note: working mechanism

Why it matters

It matters because human-in-the-loop review only has value when the human has a concrete workstation for inspecting sources, uncertainty, assumptions, structured analytic technique outputs, and reasoning paths rather than a generic prompt window.

Record note: professional value

What is already there

All nine pages render at their intended fidelity.

Record note: current status

Each instrument page declares its expert roster and its citation count, and in private support file the eight instrument pages reference more than five hundred citations across the major intelligence-studies and AI-method discipline authorities — Heuer, George, Lowenthal, Kent, Fingar, Bullock, Pashentsev, Beebe and Pherson, alongside the ODNI, NIST, EU AI Act, and Stanford HAI corpora.

Record note: current status

The citation infrastructure is in place; what is not yet built is the source-register chain that would bind each declared citation to an admitted snippet from the underlying corpus.

Record note: current status
