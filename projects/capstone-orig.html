Qualitative Capstone - qualitative analysis of analyst response to AI-introduced corruption

A concise project summary for professional review, drawn from the maintained project record.

What this does

The submitted qualitative capstone on whether structured analyst review can catch AI-introduced evidentiary corruption.

Record note: project purpose

How it works

It uses fifty matched-pair scenarios, deliberately corrupted evidence packets, Structured Analytic Technique execution, sealed coding lanes, human decision review, triad reconciliation, APEX review rounds, severity-tier review, method standards, per-scenario re-coding notes, and all-fifty claim-level human validation to keep the claim bounded.

Record note: working mechanism

Why it matters

Its value is not that it proves AI safety generally; it shows a disciplined way to study when human analysts function as a meaningful check on AI-assisted analytic error, while stating that the fifty-two-field re-coding lane, runtime MAS factorial, and full Blue Cell execution remain deferred rather than falsely completed.
