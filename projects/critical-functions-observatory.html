Critical Functions Observatory - defensive UAS / critical-infrastructure research lab

A concise project summary for professional review, drawn from the maintained project record.

What this does

A defensive UAS and critical-infrastructure research observatory built around national critical functions rather than generic drone security.

Record note: project purpose

How it works

It routes malicious-UAS and infrastructure risk through a function-first defensive-research model: CISA National Critical Functions, FAA Remote ID, White House guidance, DOJ/DHS/FAA/FCC legal-advisory constraints, OT asset inventory, DOE/EPA-relevant function dependencies, semantic graphs, provenance vaulting, publication forge outputs, and recomputable projection logic.

Record note: working mechanism

It also guards against turning defensive analysis into operational attack guidance by filtering timing, payload, and bypass details.

Record note: working mechanism

Why it matters

It matters because critical-infrastructure defense should begin with the function being protected, the legal and operational constraints around intervention, the projection engine that can be recomputed, and the evidence path that supports each protective claim.
