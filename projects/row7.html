What It Is

A local research-development application with two linked halves: cross-field mechanism discovery and dependency-aware academic workflow.

How It Works

The discovery side searches for transferable mechanisms from other fields and tests whether they survive context, language, timing, and analogy limits; Observatory, Scanner, Bridge, Cross-Pollination Lab, Assay Lab, Crucible, Incubator, Forge, and the Galaxy Map make those cross-field mechanism relations visible.

Source field: project blueprint mechanism field

The academic side carries problem exploration, problem statement, literature review, methodology, analysis, discussion, conclusion, and editing through stage rooms with instruments and standards; upstream changes can mark downstream stages stale, and downstream revisions can force earlier sections back into review.

Why It Matters

This is the distinctive Nexus claim: it is both an originality engine and a dependency-method-bound research pipeline, helping a researcher generate non-obvious ideas while keeping the eventual paper aligned from problem statement through final argument.
