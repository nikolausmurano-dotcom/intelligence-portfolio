Structured Techniques Toolkit - authoring and refinement workspace for disciplined analytic instruments

A concise project summary for professional review, drawn from the maintained project record.

What this does

A studio for converting structured analytic techniques into usable instruments.

Record note: project purpose

How it works

Each technique needs purpose, inputs, steps, failure modes, interpretation rules, reviewer actions, and example outputs, including adaptations for human analysts, AI systems, and human-AI collaboration.

Record note: working mechanism

The studio supports authoring brand-new SATs, refining and tweaking existing SATs, binding SATs to a corpus, cross-referencing the Analyst Workstation SAT surfaces, and reconciling Beebe/Pherson-style handbooks and tradecraft primers into usable method records.

Record note: working mechanism

Why it matters

It shows method-building skill: not just applying ACH or red-team ideas, but turning every SAT into a reusable, testable research and review tool with explicit inputs, operating rules, and failure modes.

Record note: professional value
