Comparative Genocide Lab - comparative analysis of mass political violence across ten canonical dimensions

A concise project summary for professional review, drawn from the maintained project record.

What this does

A comparative genocide analysis chamber built around ten canonical dimensions of mass political violence.

Record note: project purpose

How it works

It uses six zones: Charter Wall, Zone II Dimensional Spine, Zone III Case Forge, Method Chamber with coding rules, Causal and Typological Observatory, and Campaign Table.

Record note: working mechanism

The lab distinguishes concepts from operational rules, preserves the ten canonical analytic dimensions without contradiction, standardizes case cards, preserves contested-case protocols, supports first case-forge work, cross-case observatory analysis, and sprint and private support file status, and is designed to match MuranoOS-style room logic through React/TypeScript and Rust/Tauri implementation paths.

Record note: working mechanism

Why it matters

It matters because comparative genocide analysis needs dimensions, cases, methods, evidence, process, and outputs linked together; otherwise cases become isolated moral descriptions rather than comparable analytical objects.
