Defense Doctrine - defensive retrieval method discipline laboratory (claim-limit discipline)

A concise project summary for professional review, drawn from the maintained project record.

What this does

A defensive-RAG method discipline doctrine whose first job is to stop immature security claims from inflating themselves.

Record note: project purpose

How it works

Every claim must carry mission profile, attacker tier, objective class, evidence packet, current boundary, residual risk, and downgrade triggers before it can mature through C1 Designed, C2 Implemented, C3 Validated, C4 Adaptively Stress-Tested, C5 Operationally Sustainable, and C6 Externally Defensible stages, with NIST AI RMF, NIST SP, and Google SAIF-style controls as reference discipline.

Record note: working mechanism

Why it matters

Its value is the language firewall: it makes words like secure, robust, solved, government-grade, and production-ready unavailable until evidence earns them.
