# Proof Demo Cases: Nexus / Discovery Engine

**Project ID.** `row7`

**Proof tier.** file-backed-proof-build

**Public claim.** A local research-development application with two linked halves: cross-field mechanism discovery and dependency-aware academic workflow.

**Distinctive mechanism.** The discovery side searches for transferable mechanisms from other fields and tests whether they survive context, language, timing, and analogy limits; Observatory, Scanner, Bridge, Cross-Pollination Lab, Assay Lab, Crucible, Incubator, Forge, and the Galaxy Map make those cross-field mechanism relations visible. The academic side carries problem exploration, problem statement, literature review, methodology, analysis, discussion, conclusion, and editing through stage rooms with instruments and standards; upstream changes can mark downstream stages stale, and downstream revisions can force.

**Component demo cases.** 101

**Claim ceiling.** The page may claim proof-structured components and visible verification targets. It may not claim external validation, production deployment, or completed empirical findings unless the project-specific record states those facts.

## Flagship Demonstrations

### Nexus cross-field mechanism transfer

**Why distinctive.** Most search systems retrieve adjacent terms. This demo asks whether the system can carry a mechanism from one field into another while preserving fit pressure, analogy limits, and downstream research-workflow consequences.

**Runbook.**

- Enter the question into Nexus Intake and Observatory.
- Use Galaxy Map to locate non-obvious adjacent mechanism fields rather than keyword-neighbor papers only.
- Create Bridge mapping from mixed-speed air-defense logic to retrieval poisoning.
- Run XPol fit pressure: timing, resource asymmetry, false analogy, and boundary conditions.
- Route surviving hypothesis into Forge with a claim ceiling and stale-state dependencies.

**Expected evidence.**

- Galaxy neighborhood or transfer-route record
- Bridge mapping with at least one rejected false analogy
- XPol fit-pressure record
- Forge-ready research object with source and stale-state links

**Acceptance criteria.**

- The final hypothesis is not a mere metaphor; it names a transferable mechanism.
- At least one analogy limit is explicitly rejected or bounded.
- A downstream stage records what must be rechecked if the problem statement or method changes.

### Nexus academic upstream/downstream stale-state propagation

**Why distinctive.** The value is not only moving through research stages; it is forcing later stages to reopen when earlier foundations change.

**Runbook.**

- Create a project with a problem statement, methodology, and analysis stage.
- Change the problem statement after later stages exist.
- Inspect which stages become stale or review-required.
- Attempt Forge release before stale stages are resolved.

**Expected evidence.**

- Stale-stage flags
- Blocked or narrowed release decision
- Dependency map showing why later sections require review

**Acceptance criteria.**

- A downstream stage cannot silently remain release-ready after a material upstream change.
- The system distinguishes minor wording edits from changes affecting method or findings.
