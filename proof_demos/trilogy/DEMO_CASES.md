# Proof Demo Cases: Book Trilogy

**Project ID.** `trilogy`

**Proof tier.** specified-proof-plan-needs-demo

**Public claim.** A governed three-book manuscript system, not a dashboard of chapter counts.

**Distinctive mechanism.** The trilogy is organized across restraint, democratic formation, and repair. ReaderEdition chapters provide the public rewrite target; FinalProof preserves argument backbone; ConstructionKits govern chapter shape; PassageTargets supply sentence pressure and source-bearing formulations; the Master Implementation Workbook governs bridge, dependency, and terminology logic; Democratic Dialectic supplies selective voice material, especially for Public Book II.

**Component demo cases.** 11

**Claim ceiling.** The page may claim proof-structured components and visible verification targets. It may not claim external validation, production deployment, or completed empirical findings unless the project-specific record states those facts.

## Flagship Demonstrations

### Trilogy chapter-upgrade dossier

**Why distinctive.** The proof must show chapter-level governance: title, visual, argument backbone, voice register, source state, and handoff.

**Runbook.**

- Select one chapter.
- Recover argument backbone from FinalProof.
- Compare ReaderEdition flattening against ConstructionKit requirements.
- Apply book-specific register and visual/title specificity gate.
- Produce a rewrite dossier and next rewrite decision.

**Expected evidence.**

- Canonical/public chapter identity
- Argument backbone
- ReaderEdition deficit list
- Title and visual specificity decision
- Rewrite action and handoff

**Acceptance criteria.**

- The title and visual could not plausibly fit any other chapter.
- The rewrite decision preserves argument and book register.
- The dossier states what is built and what remains to rewrite.
