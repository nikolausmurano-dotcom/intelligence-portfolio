# Proof Build: Russia Security Desk

**Project ID.** `native-russia-security-desk`

**Proof tier.** specified-proof-plan-needs-demo

**Public claim.** The Russia Desk is a two-page analytical workbench: a contemporary security desk for regime security, military adaptation, influence operations, Ukraine, NATO, and strategic decision-making; and a history page for the longer imperial, Soviet, post-Soviet, and European-order inheritance that gives present judgment depth.

**Distinctive mechanism.** The mechanism is source-lane to judgment-lane conversion. Internal workbench files, Kindle/Routledge/source library source families, journal and transcript manifests, marker packs, visual dossiers, exact-source queues, and source lineage bridges are separated by role.

**Use value.** It links regional knowledge to analytic method by preserving source lineage, strategic frame, adversary logic, and claim limits for later Russia/security work.

**Minimum public demo.** show one claim moving through source lane, variable assignment, alternative review, and public claim restraint

**Claim ceiling.** The page may claim proof-structured components and visible verification targets. It may not claim external validation, production deployment, or completed empirical findings unless the project-specific record states those facts.

## Flagship Proof Builds

### Russia Desk analytic-variable proof

- Claim: The desk turns Russia material into analytic variables rather than generic commentary.
- Input: source lane, regime-security evidence, military evidence, influence evidence, and historical inheritance
- Operation: separate variables, test alternatives, bound confidence, and state public claim limits
- Expected output: structured Russia assessment with source lane, variable, alternative, and confidence boundary
- Verification check: show one claim moving through source lane, variable assignment, alternative review, and public claim restraint
