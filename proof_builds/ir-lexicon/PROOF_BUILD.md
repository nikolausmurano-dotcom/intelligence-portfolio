# Proof Build: IR Lexicon — coined-term lexicon for modern foreign-affairs discourse

**Project ID.** `ir-lexicon`

**Proof tier.** source-backed-proof-build

**Public claim.** A coined-term lexicon for modern foreign-affairs discourse.

**Distinctive mechanism.** It moves 400 raw coinages through a lacuna atlas, adoptability shortlist, 20-term canon, 10 mini-theories, 16-dimension adoption scoring, collision-risk flags, and a 25-rule propagation doctrine. Terms such as Credibility Underwriting Gap, Compellence Overdraft, Ambiguity Rent, Sanction Saturation, Standards Entrenchment, Compliance Chokepoints, and Doctrine Lock-In are treated as analytic instruments rather than slogans.

**Use value.** It matters because IR language can become too vague to do analytic work; this project shows how a term earns use through definition, diagnostic power, adoption logic, and essay/briefing application.

**Minimum public demo.** Open or inspect the local runtime path for IR Lexicon — coined-term lexicon for modern foreign-affairs discourse Runtime Or Interface Surface; confirm a room, action, or state record traces to the governing contract.

**Claim ceiling.** The page may claim proof-structured components and visible verification targets. It may not claim external validation, production deployment, or completed empirical findings unless the project-specific record states those facts.
