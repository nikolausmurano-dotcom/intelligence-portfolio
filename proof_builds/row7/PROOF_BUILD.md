# Proof Build: Discovery Engine — eight-stage cross-disciplinary research-question discovery apparatus

**Project ID.** `row7`

**Proof tier.** file-backed-proof-build

**Public claim.** A local research-development application with two linked halves: cross-field mechanism discovery and dependency-aware academic workflow.

**Distinctive mechanism.** The discovery side searches for transferable mechanisms from other fields and tests whether they survive context, language, timing, and analogy limits; Observatory, Scanner, Bridge, Cross-Pollination Lab, Assay Lab, Crucible, Incubator, Forge, and the Galaxy Map make those cross-field mechanism relations visible. The academic side carries problem exploration, problem statement, literature review, methodology, analysis, discussion, conclusion, and editing through stage rooms with instruments and standards; upstream changes can mark downstream stages stale, and downstream revisions can force.

**Use value.** This is the distinctive Nexus claim: it is both an originality engine and a dependency-governed research pipeline, helping a researcher generate non-obvious ideas while keeping the eventual paper aligned from problem statement through final argument.

**Minimum public demo.** show one initial research question entering Observatory/Galaxy, one Bridge mapping, one XPol pressure test, and one Forge-ready output with stale-state rules visible

**Claim ceiling.** The page may claim proof-structured components and visible verification targets. It may not claim external validation, production deployment, or completed empirical findings unless the project-specific record states those facts.

## Flagship Proof Builds

### Cross-field mechanism transfer demonstration

- Claim: Nexus is not ordinary keyword search; it is designed to find transferable mechanisms across fields and route them into testable research objects.
- Input: one seed research problem, at least two source fields, and candidate mechanism families
- Operation: use the Galaxy Map / Cross-Field Mechanism Atlas, Bridge mapping, and XPol fit pressure to move from source-field pattern to target-field research question
- Expected output: transfer route, fit-pressure record, rejected false analogy, and draftable research object
- Verification check: show one initial research question entering Observatory/Galaxy, one Bridge mapping, one XPol pressure test, and one Forge-ready output with stale-state rules visible

### Upstream/downstream stale-state demonstration

- Claim: The academic pipeline is wired so changes to problem statement, method, evidence, or analysis force downstream review instead of silently leaving later prose stale.
- Input: a project with at least problem statement, literature review, methodology, analysis, and draft stages
- Operation: change one upstream stage and verify that dependent stages become review-required
- Expected output: dependency warning, stale-stage flags, and required revalidation tasks
- Verification check: alter one upstream decision and capture the downstream stale-state propagation path

### Forge provenance and release gate demonstration

- Claim: Nexus can convert mature findings into shareable writing only when section support and claim ceilings are visible.
- Input: one mature finding, draft lane, support sidecar, source IDs, and release matrix
- Operation: move the finding through Forge Intake, Draft, Lineage, and Release Brake
- Expected output: draft section with provenance, export permission or export block, and public claim boundary
- Verification check: run one draft through Forge and show whether export is allowed, blocked, or narrowed
