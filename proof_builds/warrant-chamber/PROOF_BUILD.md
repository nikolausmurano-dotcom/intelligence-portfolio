# Proof Build: Source & Evidence Review — tracking what supports each claim and what still needs review

**Project ID.** `warrant-chamber`

**Proof tier.** authored-proof-build-needs-demo

**Public claim.** A manuscript-centered evidence governance chamber.

**Distinctive mechanism.** Claims are inspected through a Foundational NSRP-style sourcing protocol, Claim Halo, North Doctrine, source and counter-source, warrant strength, West Dissent, judgment, South Revision, red-team pressure, certification, screen blueprint, data model, and live MuranoOS-oriented test scenarios rather than flattened into citations or comments.

**Use value.** It matters because expert writing needs to know what authorizes a claim, what contests it, what revision state carries it, what dissent has been faced, and what test would show the warrant is strong enough.

**Minimum public demo.** select one claim and show source role, warrant strength, counter-source status, and approved claim level

**Claim ceiling.** The page may claim proof-structured components and visible verification targets. It may not claim external validation, production deployment, or completed empirical findings unless the project-specific record states those facts.

## Flagship Proof Builds

### Claim-warrant-source proof

- Claim: Source & Evidence Review should show the warrant behind a claim, not only list bibliography.
- Input: one claim, cited source, source role, passage state, counter-source, and uncertainty marker
- Operation: classify the source as method, evidence, counterargument, domain context, or future-review source and set the claim ceiling
- Expected output: claim-warrant record with support status and uncertainty boundary
- Verification check: select one claim and show source role, warrant strength, counter-source status, and approved claim level

### Contradiction and unresolved-evidence proof

- Claim: The review surface should make unresolved evidence visible instead of hiding it behind polished prose.
- Input: claim, rival evidence, missing evidence, contradiction note, and public audience
- Operation: route unresolved evidence to narrow, defer, qualify, or block the claim
- Expected output: contradiction decision and public-language constraint
- Verification check: show one claim with a contradiction or missing-source issue and the resulting public wording decision
