# Proof Build: Structured Methods Library — method-composition page across Heuer, structured analytic techniques, and intelligence methodology

**Project ID.** `analytical-methods`

**Proof tier.** specified-proof-plan-needs-demo

**Public claim.** A general analytical-methodology library: the place where method families, source authorities, and reusable tradecraft moves are catalogued and made checkable.

**Distinctive mechanism.** Four mappings define every method in the catalogue: the question the method answers, the canonical source it derives from, the analytical trap it guards against, and the way the method composes with other methods.

**Use value.** It turns analytic methods into reusable instruments with inputs, steps, failure modes, interpretation rules, and reviewer actions.

**Minimum public demo.** run one task through two candidate SAT sequences and compare diagnostic yield and burden

**Claim ceiling.** The page may claim proof-structured components and visible verification targets. It may not claim external validation, production deployment, or completed empirical findings unless the project-specific record states those facts.

## Flagship Proof Builds

### SAT sequence utility proof

- Claim: Structured techniques are not just labels; the system should show which technique or sequence fits which analytic task and where it adds value or burden.
- Input: analytic task, uncertainty type, evidence state, candidate SATs, and analyst burden
- Operation: select and sequence SATs, record rationale, and compare output quality against burden
- Expected output: technique-sequence recommendation with warning about misuse or low value
- Verification check: run one task through two candidate SAT sequences and compare diagnostic yield and burden
