# Proof Build: Defense Doctrine — defensive retrieval governance laboratory (claim-limit discipline)

**Project ID.** `row5`

**Proof tier.** source-backed-proof-build

**Public claim.** A defensive-RAG governance doctrine whose first job is to stop immature security claims from inflating themselves.

**Distinctive mechanism.** Every claim must carry mission profile, attacker tier, objective class, evidence packet, current boundary, residual risk, and downgrade triggers before it can mature through C1 Designed, C2 Implemented, C3 Validated, C4 Adaptively Stress-Tested, C5 Operationally Sustainable, and C6 Externally Defensible stages, with NIST AI RMF, NIST SP, and Google SAIF-style controls as reference discipline.

**Use value.** Its value is the language firewall: it makes words like secure, robust, solved, government-grade, and production-ready unavailable until evidence earns them.

**Minimum public demo.** Run one corpus query or source-admission fixture through Defense Doctrine — defensive retrieval governance laboratory (claim-limit discipline) Artifact Identity Register; confirm source identity, retrieval path, support state, and output: what the packet is.

**Claim ceiling.** The page may claim proof-structured components and visible verification targets. It may not claim external validation, production deployment, or completed empirical findings unless the project-specific record states those facts.
